Mating

Jellyfish lifecycle isnâ€™t linear, and reproduction can be both asexual and sexual. At an early, post-larval stage, the polyp stage, it can asexually reproduce by cloning itself. This produces a larval stage which can itself advance to the polyp stage and continue this part of the cycle.

Sexual reproduction happens in the mature, medusa, stage of a jellyfishes lifecycle. Both male and female jellyfish release eggs and sperm into the area, depending on their diet and in some species the time of day. The released genetic material meets in the water and forms larval planula, which anchor themselves to the bottom of a structure until they mature into a polyp.
